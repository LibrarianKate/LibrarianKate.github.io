Hello and welcome! I'm Kate Coleman. I am a Collection and Database Management librarian in the St. Louis, Missouri area focusing on cataloging, resource sharing, and library consortium management. Technical Services is a vital part of any library, and I am passionate about sharing my knowledge regarding its integral part in librarianship.

About me

I started my Technical Services career in 2002 and have stuck with it ever since. I have a passion for cataloging, metadata, and resource sharing and am a proud member of the Evergreen ILS open-source community. Within my own state-wide consortium, I am the cataloging chairperson, in charge of onboarding and training incoming cataloging staff. I feel strongly that both customer service and leadership styles are an art form. In my free time, I enjoy spending time with my husband and daughter. We spend a lot of time outdoors, kayaking, hiking and biking. I'm usually listening to an audiobook while I do these activities, or at the very least have a well-worn paperback in my backpack for stops.

Educational background

  • Bachelor of Science, Business Management - University of Missouri, St. Louis
  • Master of Library and Information Science - University of Missouri, Columbia
  • Graduate of the Dale Carnegie Leadership Institute

Experience overview

I started my career as a student worker in an academic library at the age of 17 and have worked in libraries ever since. I joined the ranks of behind-the-scenes librarianship in 2002, starting out as a Technical Services clerk. Over the following 17 years, I worked my way up the ladder to currently heading my department as a Technical Services Specialist. I am slated to become part of the management team in September of 2022.

Skill set:

  • RDA cataloging
  • Acquisitions administration
  • Collection management
  • Customer service
  • Data management
